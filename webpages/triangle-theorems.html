Triangle Theorems

Explore the fundamental theorems that govern the properties and relationships of triangles.

Triangle Similarity Theorems

Two triangles are similar if their corresponding angles are equal and their corresponding sides are proportional. This is established through several theorems:

  • AA (Angle-Angle): If two angles of one triangle are congruent to two angles of another triangle, the triangles are similar.
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): If one angle of a triangle is equal to one angle of another triangle, and the sides including these angles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.
  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): If the corresponding sides of two triangles are proportional, then the triangles are similar.

Triangle Congruence Theorems

Two triangles are congruent if all corresponding sides and angles are equal. The following theorems are used to prove congruence:

  • SSS (Side-Side-Side): If three sides of one triangle are equal to three sides of another triangle, they are congruent.
  • SAS (Side-Angle-Side): If two sides and the included angle of one triangle are equal to two sides and the included angle of another triangle, they are congruent.
  • ASA (Angle-Side-Angle): If two angles and the included side of one triangle are equal to two angles and the included side of another triangle, they are congruent.
  • AAS (Angle-Angle-Side): If two angles and a non-included side of one triangle are equal to two angles and a corresponding non-included side of another triangle, they are congruent.

Pythagorean Theorem

The Pythagorean theorem states that in a right-angled triangle, the square of the length of the hypotenuse is equal to the sum of the squares of the other two sides.

Triangle Inequality Theorem

The triangle inequality theorem states that the sum of the lengths of any two sides of a triangle must be greater than the length of the third side.
