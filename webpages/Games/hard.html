Draw squares (CBDE), (BAGF), and (ACIH) on sides (BC), (AB), and (AC) of a right-angled triangle (ABC) with the right angle at (C).
From point (A), draw a line parallel to (BD) and (CE), intersecting (BC) at (K) and (DE) at (L).
Triangles (BCF) and (BDA) are formed.
Triangles (BDA) and (BCF) are congruent.
Rectangle (BDLK) is twice the area of triangle (BDA), and rectangle (CKLE) is twice the area of triangle (BCF).
Square (BAGF) is equal in area to rectangle (BDLK); Square (ACIH) is equal in area to rectangle (CKLE).
The total area of squares (BAGF) and (ACIH) is equal to the area of square (CBDE).
Thus, (AB^2 + AC^2 = BC^2).
Definition of squares based on triangle sides.
Construction based on parallel postulate.
Figures resulted from the previous construction.
Side-angle-side postulate: two sides and the included angle of one triangle are congruent to two sides and the included angle of another triangle.
The area of a triangle is half the area of a rectangle on the same base and having the same altitude.
The area of the squares on the legs of the triangle is equivalent to the areas of the corresponding rectangles formed by construction.
Summation of areas based on the equivalence established by congruent triangles and the properties of the constructed rectangles.
Concluding from the equality of the areas, the Pythagorean theorem is proved.
