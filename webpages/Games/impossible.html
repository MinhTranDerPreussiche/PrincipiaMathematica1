Match Each Integration Step with Its Explanation

Statements

Consider the function cosine of x over the interval from 0 to pi.
The area A under the curve from x = 0 to x = pi is given by the integral from 0 to pi of cosine of x, dx.
Integrate cosine of x from 0 to pi.
The integral of cosine of x, dx, equals sine of x plus a constant C.
Evaluate sine of x from 0 to pi.
Sine of pi minus sine of 0 equals 0 minus 0 equals 0.
Therefore, A equals 0.

Reasons

Given: The function and interval are specified.
Definition of Area under a Curve: The area under a curve from a to b is given by the integral of the function over that interval.
Integral Calculus: To find the area under the curve, compute the definite integral of the function.
Antiderivative: The antiderivative of cosine of x is sine of x, plus a constant C.
Fundamental Theorem of Calculus: Apply the evaluated antiderivative at the upper and lower limits of the integral.
Substitution: Sine of pi equals 0 and sine of 0 equals 0.
Conclusion: The integral of cosine of x from 0 to pi results in an area of 0.
