Prove interior angles of triangles add up to 180 degrees

Statements

angle CAB = 0 degrees, contradiction.
Use Alternate Interior Angles Theorem: angle ACB = angle ACD and angle BCA = angle BCE.
Substitute equal angles: angle ACB + angle BCA = 180 degrees.
Add angle CAB: angle ACB + angle BCA + angle CAB = 180 degrees + angle CAB.
Assume angle A + angle B + angle C is not equal to 180 degrees.
Construct triangle ABC.
Draw line DE through C parallel to AB.
DE is straight, so angle ACD + angle BCE = 180 degrees.

Reasons

Construction for parallel lines.
Angle sum property of a straight line.
For contradiction.
Substituting the equal angles shows the sum is 180 degrees.
Adding the third angle proves all angles equal 180 degrees plus the additional angle, leading to contradiction.
Substitution of equal angles based on parallel line properties.
Contradicts the nature of a non-degenerate triangle. Triangle angles must sum to 180 degrees.
